Stars

Mika: In 2016 we added a way to indicate queerness behind the camera too.

Stars

A system to recognize when shows are made for us.

Mika: Before you get mad, it's a play on the (problematic) "Gold Star" Lesbian concept.

Gold

Openly queer creators with a majority queer characters, out queer actors and stories made for a queer female audience.

Examples: The L Word, Take My Wife and Pose.

Mika: Gold star here means you're super duper queer, in front of and behind the camera, making things for queer female viewers.

Silver

Openly queer creators with queer characters and stories made for a queer female audience. May have no out queer actors, but has a significant number of stories geared towards a non-queer female audience.

Examples: Queer As Folk, Steven Universe and RED.

Mika: The silver star is still EXTRA queer. They're aimed at queers in general, but not exclusively.

Bronze

Openly queer creators, some queer characters and stories OR made for a general audience. Also used for shows focused on a queer main character and their stories, but has no out queers involved with production or acting.

Examples: Sense8, American Horror Story and Janet King.

Mika: The Bronze Star is for queer shows that are made for everyone. I like to point at Sense8 here, becuase it's a great show, made by wonderful, talented, queer people, but it's not a queer show like Queer as Folk. It's for EVERYONE on purpose.

Character Scores Formula

( ( Regulars × 3 ) + Recurring + ( Guests / 2 ) ) + ( Queer IRL × 2 ) + ( Transgender IRL × 2 ) - ( Dead × 3 )

Mika: Math nerds, rejoice! You may notice, you can have a negative transgender score if the majority of your transgender characters are NOT played by transgender actors. If you have 4 transgender characters and all are played by cis actors, you would actually have a -4 times 2, for -8 points.

Character Scores Formula

  • Movies - score ÷ 2
  • Mini Series - score ÷ 1.5
  • Web Series - score ÷ 1.25
Mika: The reason we adjust things here is because we grade on a curve. It's EASIER for web series and TV movies to include queer characters, so they need to work a little harder to be as awesome as a show that puts on 20 episodes a year.

Show Tropes

  • None
  • Good: Happy Ending, Everyone’s Queer
  • Maybe: Coming Out, Big Queer Wedding
  • Ploy: Erased Queerness, Happiness then Tragedy, Subtext Only, Queer For Ratings, Queer for Laughs
  • Bad: Big Bad Queers, Prison, Queerbaiting, Queerbashing
  • Regular: Everything else
Mika: And now the other crazy math section. We put the tropes into six categories, some good and some bad. If a show has no tropes, it gets the most points, followed by all good, and so on and so forth. But then we play math games with a formula.

Show Tropes Formula

( ( Good + Maybe - Ploy - Bad ) ÷ ( Good + Maybe + Ploy + Bad ) ) x 100

Mika: The bonus for ploy tropes is that if you don't have any, you get extra points. This helps balance out the mediocrity of most shows, where they may do bad things but they're NOT doing it just for ratings. Oh and if you kill anyone, you loose 1/3rd of your score.

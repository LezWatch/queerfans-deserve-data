Stars

In 2016 we added Stars. A play on the "Gold Star" Lesbian thing.

Stars

A star system to rank the queerness level of a show.

A play on the "Gold Star" Lesbian thing.

Gold

Openly queer creators, majority queer characters, out queer actors and stories made for a queer female audience.

Examples: The L Word, Take My Wife and Pose.

All queer.

Silver

Openly queer creators, queer characters and stories made for a queer female audience, but may have no out queer actors or also has a significant number of stories geared towards a non-queer female audience.

Examples: Queer As Folk, Steven Universe and RED.

Bronze

Openly queer creators, some queer characters and stories, made for a general audience. Also used for shows focused on a queer main character and their stories, but has no out queers involved with production or acting.

Examples: Sense8, American Horror Story and Janet King.

Show Scores

In October 2017 we added show scoring to the site. (Mika, put on a nerdy jacket here)

Show Scores

A mathematical method of a generating a numeric score for TV shows from 0 to 100 to help identify shows that are ‘better’ for queer audiences than others.

That summer, Mika thought the first formula for scoring while waiting in line for a taping of One Day at a Time. People probably thought she was nuts.

Show Scores

Combine the quantitative with the qualitative

  • Ratings
  • Character Survival Rate
  • Character Score
  • Show Tropes
We broke the 'important' parts of a show into four sections to undertand the value of representation. This has a lot of crazy math to explain it, but we're going to give you context first.

Ratings

  • Ratings: Realness, Quality, Screentime
  • Worth Watching: Thumbs up, down, meh
  • Stars: Gold, Silver, Bronze
  • Trigger Warnings: Low, Medium, High
  • Show We Love: Yes or No
The concept of 'ratings' was really just applying everything we already knew about a show and giving it a numerical value. Ratings we already gave 0-5, the thumbs were only negative if a thumb-down, stars are always a plus, and trggers are always a minus. If we love a show, it gets extra points.

Character Survival Rate

The percentage of queer characters who are alive.

This is the easy one. Everyone alive = 100%, everyone dead = 0%, everything else is just a straight up percentage.

Character Scores

  • Roles - What's the balance of guests to regulars
  • Clichés - No Cliches (or just Queer IRL) is good
  • Death - Dead characters are bad
  • Trans Representation - Are transgender characters playing characters?
  • Show Type - Expectations of web vs series
      • It's messy here, but we're trying to come to terms with how GOOD character rep is, relative to the different stakes in a TV movie versus a full series. We use death again to account for people like Sara Lance who give a SHOW a death flag, but not her character.

Character Scores Forumla

( ( Regulars × 3 ) + Recurring + ( Guests / 2 ) ) + ( Queer IRL × 2 ) - ( Dead × 3 ) + ( Transgender IRL × 2 )

Math nerds, rejoice! Now keep in mind, you can have a negative transgender score if the majority of your transgender characters are not played by transgender actors. So if you have 4 transgender characters and all are played by cis actors, you would actually have a -4 times 2, for -8 points.

Show Tropes

  • None
  • Good: Happy Ending, Everyone’s Queer
  • Maybe: Coming Out, Big Queer Wedding
  • Ploy: Erased Queerness, Happiness then Tragedy, Subtext Only, Queer For Ratings, Queer for Laughs
  • Bad: Big Bad Queers, Prison, Queerbaiting, Queerbashing
  • Regular: Everything else
We put the tropes into six categories, some good and some bad. If a show has no tropes, it gets the most points, followed by all good, and so on and so forth. But then we play math games with a forumla.

Show Tropes Formula

( Good Trops + Maybe Tropes - Bad Tropes ) ÷ Number of Tropes + Ploy Bonus

The bonus for ploy tropes is that if you don't have any, you get extra points. This helps balance out the medicotrity of most shows, where they may do bad things but they're not doing it just for ratings. Oh and if you kill anyone, you loose 1/3rd of your score.

Bonus Points!

Intersectionality Good!

For every intersecionality aspect we award, a show gets extra points. There's a max limit to that of course. All those scores get tossed into a barrel and come out between 0 and 100.
That was a lot, right? I have actual video of Mika coding the score formulas.

The Future of Representation

Even though the numbers can be depressing, I'm an optimist, and I am hopeful for the future.

Black Lightning

First black lesbian superhero main character.

Black Lightning established strong representation from the start. And raised the bar by not being timid about Anissa's sexuality. And they also...
Made her bulletproof. Not canceled. We're getting a third season, to boot!

Steven Universe

First same sex female proposal and wedding on a children's show.

I love SU so much, and the fact that this happened on a show for children on the Cartoon Network gives me a lot of hope for the future. Announced at SDCC renewed for season 6.

Vida

A show featuring Latinx queer women, created, written and produced by Latinx queer women.

Let's admire this shot of Emma. Out Latinx women were involved in every aspect of this show resulting in quality stories. We're getting a season two and MORE episodes than season 1.

Pose

A show about transgender women of color with the highest number of out transgender and queer actors, writers and producers.

With queer folks involved in every aspect of this show we got authentic, quality representation. Renewed for season 2 which will air in 2019.

Supergirl

First transgender superhero. The role will be played by out transgender actress Nicole Maines.

Nicole being added to the cast of season 5 was announced at SDCC. I'll let her speak for herself on the importance of this role.

Batwoman

The first titular superhero main character to helm a show, and be openly queer from day one as character and actor.

Titular = a show where the title is the name of the character.
They have chosen Ruby Rose to play her. They will introduce her in the Arrowverse crossover and then she'll get her own show.
